# WhatsApp Advanced Analytics — Intelligence Domains

**Executive review · 2026-04-18**

Every metric we expose for WhatsApp analytics is grouped into **seven Intelligence Domains**. Each domain answers a specific question a WhatsApp marketer already asks on a Monday morning. The same taxonomy powers the AI chip categories and agent-mode prompts in the product, so strategy, metrics, and UX all speak one language.

## Intelligence Domains × KPIs × Marketer Jobs × Critical Questions

| # | Intelligence Domain | Key KPIs | Marketer's Job-to-be-Done | Critical Analytics Question |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Reach & Deliverability** | Sent · Delivery rate · Failure by reason · Read rate · Read latency · Quality rating trend · Messaging-limit utilization · Country-level delivery variation | Protect my sending reputation and audience reach | *"Will my message still be delivered tomorrow at today's sending pace?"* |
| 2 | **Engagement & Interaction** | CTR · Unique clickers · Button / list selection rate · Carousel and product-card engagement · Reply rate · Median time to first reply | Prove my creative is actually working | *"Which element — subject, button, list option, product card — is doing the work?"* |
| 3 | **Conversation Operations** | Bot containment rate · Human handoff rate · Resolution rate · Avg time to resolution · CSAT · Intent distribution · Unresolved backlog | Keep conversation quality high without growing the team | *"Where is the bot breaking down, and which intents must escalate to a human?"* |
| 4 | **Flow & Task Completion** | Flow start / completion / timeout rates · Step-level drop-off · Retry rate · Completion by segment, geo, and template version | Find and fix friction in the journey | *"Which step is losing customers — and for whom?"* |
| 5 | **Revenue & Attribution** | Conversions · Revenue (direct + assisted) · AOV · Revenue per delivered / read / click / reply / completed flow · New vs repeat revenue · Time to conversion · Incremental lift where holdouts exist | Prove the channel pays for itself | *"How much revenue did this campaign drive — directly and as part of a longer journey?"* |
| 6 | **Cost & Efficiency** | Spend · Cost per delivered / read / click / reply / conversion · Revenue-to-cost ratio · Projected spend · Cost by category and country | Scale spend without eroding margin | *"What does each conversion really cost me, and where is that slipping?"* |
| 7 | **Cross-Channel Orchestration** | WhatsApp-only vs mixed-sequence performance · Sequence order and time gap · Incremental conversion by sequence · Fatigue risk by touch count · Channel-preference propensity | Choose the right channel mix for each segment | *"Does WhatsApp alone win for this cohort, or does email → WhatsApp within 6 hours win?"* |

## Executive headline

> **Seven Intelligence Domains. Seven Monday-morning questions. One vocabulary.**
>
> Advanced WhatsApp analytics isn't a catalog of metrics — it's a set of conversations marketers already want to have, pre-structured by the product. Every KPI in the platform maps to a job a marketer is hired to do, and every insight, chip, and agent turn is ranked against these seven lenses.

## Why this structure matters

- **Adoption** — marketers recognize the questions before they learn the tool. No new vocabulary to train.
- **AI grounding** — every agent answer cites evidence from exactly one domain, so users know *why* an insight is being shown.
- **Roadmap clarity** — new metrics slot into an existing domain, or we admit we're expanding the product's surface area.
- **Competitive framing** — rivals ship metric lists; we ship answers organized by the job.

---

*Notes for the read.* The seven domains are also the seven AI chip groups in the product's Smart mode and Agent mode, plus the four-lens KPI switcher (Business · Engagement · Conversation · Cost & Quality) is a roll-up view of domains 5/2/3/6 respectively.
